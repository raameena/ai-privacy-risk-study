# AI Privacy Risk & User Behavior Study

A survey-based research project analyzing how users perceive AI privacy risks, trust AI assistants, and share sensitive information despite awareness of potential risks.

## Overview

This study examined whether users understand AI privacy risks, whether they feel at risk, and whether awareness changes their behavior.

The project used survey responses, statistical testing, and a short educational intervention to measure patterns in AI trust, risk perception, and safer AI-use behavior.

## Key Findings

- Found an awareness-to-action gap: users reported low trust in AI systems but still shared sensitive information.
- Found that 63% of frequent AI users shared at least one sensitive information type despite 62.5% reporting low trust in AI systems.
- Found that 55% of frequent AI users uploaded personal documents into AI systems at least once.
- Found that targeted education significantly improved AI verification behavior.
- Showed that short educational prompts may help users make safer decisions when using AI tools.
